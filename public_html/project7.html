BANK MARKETING CAMPAIGN PREDICTION

Predicting Term Deposit Subscription

Project Goal

To identify which customer and activity characteristics (such as call duration, financial status, and historical activity) most strongly predict term deposit subscription success.

This final project for IAT 461: Data Science for Interactive Systems analyzes 45,211 bank marketing call records, addresses the 11.7% class imbalance, and builds Logistic Regression and Decision Tree models. Completed as a two-person team, with both members fully participating in every stage of the work.

01

Exploratory Data Analysis & Challenges

Preliminary analysis revealed a strong class imbalance, with roughly a 9:1 ratio between non-subscribers and subscribers. This issue was addressed during model training using balanced class weights and stratified cross-validation. The EDA also highlighted meaningful differences across key numerical features, including call duration, account balance, and past campaign outcomes.

02

Feature Engineering & Model Preparation

To improve predictive performance, several additional features were engineered to capture seasonal patterns, contact behavior, and previous interactions. During data preparation, appropriate encoding and scaling techniques were applied based on each model's requirements.

03

Model Training & Validation

Both Logistic Regression and Decision Tree models were trained. Logistic Regression demonstrated superior stability and consistency across three validation methods (Train-Test, K-Fold, and Stratified K-Fold).
The Decision Tree model was optimized using Grid Search, although depth analysis revealed a slight tendency toward overfitting.

04

Key Feature Analysis & Quantified Insights

Both models consistently highlighted call duration as the most influential predictor of subscription.
Logistic Regression indicated that longer calls and positive prior outcomes increased the likelihood of subscribing, while certain customer attributes and frequent contact attempts were associated with lower probabilities.
The Decision Tree uncovered clear business rules as well â€” for example, customers with longer call interactions showed noticeably higher subscription rates.

05

Business Recommendations & Conclusion

The final recommendation is that the bank should prioritize a "quality over quantity" strategy. Call Quality: Set sales targets for conversations longer than 4 minutes, investing in training agents on advanced conversational skills. Customer Targeting: Strategically target customers without existing loans (e.g., housing loans) and those with tertiary education, as they are significantly more likely to subscribe. Dual-Model Strategy: Use Logistic Regression for precise customer scoring and prioritization, while using the Decision Tree's intuitive rules to guide operational decisions.
